This page is the later-management surface for visitors who want to grant continuity consent after declining it, or revoke continuity consent after enabling it earlier from the Liaison Keyboard banner.

Policy scope

Applies to liaisonkeyboard.com and app.liaisonkeyboard.com.

Default stance

Optional continuity storage remains off until you explicitly enable it.

Last updated

March 25, 2026.

Preference center

Manage continuity consent

Necessary storage stays on for core site behavior. Optional continuity storage only controls whether Liaison Keyboard remembers referral, scenario, and dashboard handoff context between the marketing site and the app.

Revoking continuity consent clears saved referral and session-handoff hints such as lk_deeplink and liaison_auth_hint.

What changes

Continuity storage only

  • Allows lightweight referral and scenario handoff context to persist across visits.
  • Lets the site remember whether to show a dashboard return state for a known session hint.
  • Does not expose conversation content on the marketing site.
What stays the same

Necessary storage remains active

  • Core page rendering and basic navigation continue to work.
  • The site still respects your most recent saved cookie decision.
  • The homepage banner remains the first-choice consent surface for first-time visitors.
Stored context

Attribution and session handoff

Liaison Keyboard may store lightweight referral or scenario identifiers so a visitor who starts on the landing site can continue into the app without losing context. Typical values include utm_source, ref_id, scenario_id, and prefill.

Related privacy controls

What this page does not control

  • Full Memory remains a privacy-first opt-in feature controlled inside the app.
  • Billing data and subscription management are handled through Stripe.
  • Support questions can still be sent to support@liaisonkeyboard.com.
