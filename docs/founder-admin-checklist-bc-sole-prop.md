# Founder Admin Checklist (BC Sole Proprietor)

This checklist is tailored to the current setup:

- Jurisdiction: British Columbia, Canada
- Business form: sole proprietor operating under a business name
- Product stage: pre-revenue software product
- CRA status: business number already obtained
- BC status: name request pending

Use this as an operating checklist, not legal or tax advice. Mark each item with
an owner and completion date as work progresses.

## Current Snapshot

| Item | Current state | Action status |
|---|---|---|
| Sole proprietor structure | Chosen | Keep |
| Business name | Pending BC approval | Track until approved or revise |
| CRA business number | Obtained | Confirm program accounts needed |
| Product revenue | None yet | No immediate GST collection unless required |
| Team | Founder-operated | Keep contractor documentation clean if others contribute |

## Immediate Actions

### 1. Lock legal identity details

- Record the exact legal name of the proprietor.
- Record the exact proposed business name submitted in the BC name request.
- Save the BC name request number, submission date, and expiry or response window.
- Decide now whether the business can operate temporarily only under the legal
  personal name if the business name is delayed or rejected.

### 2. Build the core admin folder

Create and maintain a single founder admin folder with:

- BC name request records
- CRA business number notice
- ID verification and address records used for registrations
- banking records
- contracts and contractor agreements
- software vendor agreements and invoices
- bookkeeping exports
- annual tax working papers

Recommended subfolders:

- `registrations/`
- `tax/`
- `banking/`
- `contracts/`
- `vendors/`
- `insurance/`
- `privacy-security/`

### 3. Decide what the CRA BN is for

Confirm which CRA program accounts are actually active under the business number:

- `RT` for GST/HST
- `RP` for payroll
- `RC` for corporate income tax
- import/export accounts if ever needed

For a sole proprietor, a business number can exist before all program accounts are
needed. Verify that only the required accounts are open.

### 4. Set up bookkeeping before first revenue

- Open a dedicated business bank account if the bank permits the current status.
- If the bank requires the registered business name first, keep a dated note and
  use a clean interim tracking method until registration is complete.
- Separate business and personal spending immediately.
- Choose bookkeeping software or a spreadsheet ledger now.
- Create categories for:
  - domain and hosting
  - software subscriptions
  - contractor costs
  - design and marketing
  - legal and accounting
  - hardware and equipment
  - founder reimbursements

### 5. Track startup costs from day one

- Capture every receipt and invoice.
- Record who paid, when, and whether reimbursement is owed.
- Tag pre-launch costs separately from post-launch operating costs.
- Keep a short description for each expense so the business purpose is obvious
  later.

## BC Registration Path

### 6. After the name request is approved

- Complete the BC sole proprietorship business registration under the approved
  business name.
- Save the registration confirmation and effective date.
- Check renewal dates and add them to a calendar.
- Update the exact business name everywhere it appears:
  - website footer
  - terms
  - privacy policy
  - invoices
  - vendor accounts
  - banking

### 7. If the name request is rejected or expires

- Review the rejection reason.
- Decide whether to revise and resubmit, choose a new name, or operate under the
  legal personal name for a period.
- Do not publish the rejected name as if registration is complete.

## Tax and CRA Checklist

### 8. GST/HST decision

Key practical point: pre-revenue does not automatically remove GST obligations,
but many very small businesses do not need to register until they exceed the
small supplier threshold or choose to register voluntarily.

- Confirm whether you are currently unregistered for GST/HST or already have an
  `RT` account.
- Decide whether to remain unregistered for now or register voluntarily.
- If staying unregistered for now:
  - monitor revenue carefully from the first sale
  - do not charge GST/HST until registration is actually in place
- If registering voluntarily:
  - set up invoice wording and tax handling before first sale
  - confirm filing frequency and bookkeeping treatment

### 9. Income tax treatment

- Track business income and expenses separately for sole proprietor reporting.
- Maintain a simple year-to-date profit and loss view.
- Reserve cash for income tax even before consistent revenue begins.
- Keep records that support home office, internet, phone, and equipment allocations
  if those claims may be taken.

### 10. Payroll only if hiring employees

- Do not open payroll processes unless you will actually pay employees.
- If using contractors, keep signed agreements that support contractor status.
- If later hiring employees, set up payroll before the first wage payment.

## Product and Contracting Admin

### 11. Put baseline contracts in place

- Founder template for contractor agreement
- confidentiality and IP assignment language
- advisor agreement if unpaid advisors receive any compensation or rights
- customer terms of service
- privacy policy
- acceptable use or platform rules if relevant

For this product stage, IP assignment and confidentiality should be treated as
mandatory before external contributors create meaningful product assets or code.

### 12. Confirm ownership of product assets

- Domain names are registered to the founder or business-controlled account.
- Hosting, analytics, support, and email accounts are under founder-controlled
  logins.
- Source code repository ownership and admin access are documented.
- Design files, brand assets, and app store assets are recoverable by the founder.

### 13. Build a simple cap-table substitute

As a sole proprietor there is no share structure, but you should still maintain a
record of any promises or arrangements involving:

- revenue share
- contractor bonuses
- future equity discussions if incorporation is expected later
- advisor compensation

Do not leave side arrangements informal.

## Banking, Payments, and Revenue Readiness

### 14. Prepare for the first dollar of revenue

- Decide what entity name will appear on invoices and receipts once the BC
  registration is complete.
- Confirm payment processor onboarding requirements before launch.
- Match banking name, registration name, and payment processor details as closely
  as possible.
- Draft an invoice template even if payments will mostly be automated.

### 15. Keep founder draws and reimbursements clean

- Record every founder cash injection as owner contribution.
- Record every business-paid personal cost as owner draw if not reimbursement.
- Record every personal-paid business cost as reimbursable or contributed capital,
  using one consistent method.

## Compliance and Risk Controls

### 16. Insurance review

- Decide whether to obtain at least general commercial liability and cyber/privacy
  coverage before launch or before signing any B2B customer.
- Review policy triggers tied to handling user data, subcontractors, or claims
  around service failures.

### 17. Privacy and security baseline

- Maintain a data inventory for what user data the product collects.
- Keep vendor list and processor terms together.
- Ensure privacy policy matches actual data flow.
- Maintain an incident response contact list and basic breach handling notes.

### 18. Accessibility and consumer-facing disclosures

- Ensure website legal pages are discoverable before launch.
- Verify support contact details are published.
- Verify refund, cancellation, or trial terms are clear if those models will be
  offered.

## Ongoing Founder Cadence

### Weekly

- Reconcile business spending.
- File receipts and invoices.
- Review vendor subscriptions started during development.
- Update the launch blocker list for admin items.

### Monthly

- Review cash runway.
- Review revenue status against GST/HST decision.
- Confirm business records are backed up.
- Review outstanding contracts, signatures, and renewals.

### Quarterly

- Review whether staying a sole proprietorship still makes sense.
- Review whether incorporation is justified for liability, investment, or tax
  planning reasons.
- Review privacy, terms, and vendor records against actual product changes.

## Decision Points To Revisit

These are the highest-value founder decisions still open in the current setup:

1. Whether to wait for BC business name approval before opening all external
   accounts.
2. Whether GST/HST registration should stay deferred or be completed voluntarily
   before launch.
3. Whether contractor and IP assignment paperwork is already in place for anyone
   touching the product.
4. Whether the business should remain a sole proprietorship through launch or
   convert to a corporation before revenue or outside investment.

## Minimum Done Definition Before Launch

Treat founder admin as launch-ready when all of the following are true:

- BC business registration is complete, or you have consciously chosen a lawful
  temporary operating path.
- Business banking and bookkeeping are separate from personal spending.
- Customer-facing legal documents are published and internally consistent.
- Contractor and IP ownership paperwork is signed where needed.
- Tax handling for the first sale is decided and documented.
- Payment processor and invoice identity details match the business setup.
- Core records are organized so an accountant or lawyer can review them without
  reconstructing the business from scratch.
